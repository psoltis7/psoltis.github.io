Microsoft Transact-SQL occupied my brain throughout this week. SQL is a standard language for accessing and manipulating databases. While it was nice to get a break from C#, I am ready to get back to it! SQL is not my forte and a career in data is likely not in my future.

Setting up my lab environment proved to be extremely difficult as Azure Data Studio crashed 3947293929 times in the process. SQL is a powerful tool when employed correctly and I see its value in the programming space. I will continue to strengthen my data muscles throughout the course and who knows, I might learn to like it!

On the professional development side of the house, we began to create what I like to call our “story boxes.” It is a place to store information you will use during an interview to answer behavioral questions.

With the story box ready to go we were able to craft S.T.A.R. statements:

  • Situation
  • Task
  • Action
  • Result
However, I prefer to use S.A.I.L. statements:
  • Situation
  • Action
  • Impact
  • Lesson
I learned not to get caught up in the “significance” of the story. Our lives are jam-packed with stories, it is just a matter of uncovering them. So, what’s in your story box?

Until next time amigos!

Week 1

via GIPHY

Welcome to my blog! I will be sharing short, weekly posts throughout my time with Microsoft Software and Systems Academy. I am enrolled in the Cloud Application Development path. Let’s jump right in!

Week 1 is over and I am still recovering from information overload. On Saturday, I searched for my phone for over a hour only to discover I had somehow left it in the refrigerator. I think my brain decided to take the day off.

Monday - Thursday the program consists of technical instruction. This week we focused on:

  • Core Programmming Concepts
  • Program Flow
  • Algorithms and Data Structures
  • Error Handling and Debugging
  • Introduction to Object-Oriented Programming
It is alot of information coming at you from all different angles. The key to success is to break the ice with your fellow cohort mates early! Everyone is coming into the program with varying levels of experience. And working as a team will make the struggle less frustrating.

Also, don’t be afraid to ask questions! It is likely that others are stuck and may have the same question. Better to get clarification in order to prevent you from going down the wrong rabbit hole.

The resources I use when I am confused (which is quite often) are:
  1. stack overflow
  2. Microsoft Technical Documentation
  3. W3Schools
Fridays are reserved for professional development. We spent the day learning about each other and ensuring various administrative tasks were completed. I am looking forward to resume reviews and mock interviews.

Until next week fellow humans!
